Highlights Of This Trip

  • Stunning peaks which are situated at the center of Imja, Hunku, and Barun.
  • Spectacular view of Everest, Makalu, Lhotse.
  • Perfect practices for those who want to climb Mt. Everest and any other 8000M peaks.
  • Sherpa Valley in the Khumbu Region.

Nagarkot is a scenic city located 32km from Khatmandu in, and with spectacular views of, the Kathmandu Valley. It is a widely popular hill resort with a population of about 4,500. Renowned for its spectacular views of the Himalayas, and with its close proximity to Kathmandu, Nagarkot has become a preferred weekend getaway for nature lovers. Blessed with a non-extreme climate all year round, it is a wonderful destination treat for tourists in all seasons. Summers are mostly moderate and pleasant, while winters are cold, and sometimes chilling. Monsoons make their presence felt in the period of June to September. The city has plenty to offer visitors in the form of panoramic sights, hiking, nature trails, mountain biking and more. The stunning sights of the Dhaulagiri range in the west and Mount Everest in the east are the most popular with tourists. Along the way, the historic town of Bhaktapur is a much frequented tourist spot.

Dhulikhe is an interesting small Newari town 32km from Kathmandu, not just a tourist resort. It is also a good centre for short day treks and Ideal for “refresh and relax” weekend getaways. Many visitors come here to stretch their legs before setting off on longer treks and to access easy-to-reach Himalayan range viewpoints.

Trip Outline

Day 01: Arrival in Kathmandu.
Day 02: Fly from Kathmandu to Lukla (2886m) and trek to Phakding (2640m).
Day 03: Trek from Phakding to Namche Bazaar (3440m).
Day 04: A day for rest at Namche Bazaar (3440m) for acclimatization and explore around.
Day 05: Trek from Namche Bazaar to Tengboche (3867m) takes about five hours.
Day 06: Trek from Tengboche to Dingboche (4260m) takes approximately five hours.
Day 07: Trek from Dingboche to Lobuche (4930m) takes about five and half hours.
Day 08: Trek from Lobuche to Everest Base Camp (5486m) via Gorakshep (5180m) takes about seven hours.
Day 09: : Trek from Gorakshep to Kala Patthar (5545m) and then to Pheriche (4243m) takes about six hours.
Day 10: Trek from Pheriche to Tengboche (3867m) takes approximately Four Hours.

Detail Itinerary

Day 01 Arrival in Kathmandu

Upon arrival, be met at airport and transferred to your hotel. Rest of the day at leisure. Overnight hotel

Day 02 In Kathmandu

After breakfast (included), 9.00 AM pick up from hotel for sightseeing tour of Kathmandu city (Pashupatinath, Swoyambhunath, Boudhanath and Hanuman Dhoka Palace). Kathmandu City: The capital of the Kingdom of Nepal. Here you will visit the temple of the Living Goddess, who acknowledges the greetings of the devotees from the balcony of her temple residence – Kasthamandap which is the source of the name Kathmandu and is supposedly made from the timber of a single tree. Then visit Durbar Square with it’s array of temples overlooked by the Hanuman Dhoka Palace, the ancient home of Nepalese Royalty. Swayambhunath: Located approx. 3 km from the city center, this Buddhist Stupa is said to be 2000 years old. The Stupa’s main structure is composed of a solid hemisphere of brick and earth supporting a lofty conical spire capped by a pinnacle of copper gilt. Painted on the four-sided base of the spire are the all-seeing eyes of Lord Buddha. This hill is a mosaic of small Chaityas and Pagoda temples. Overnight Hotel ; (B)

Day 03 Kathmandu – Pokhara (200Km – Approx. 7 hrs Drive)

After breakfast (included) transfer from Kathmandu to Pokhara by road – 6 hours by tourist bus. Be greeted at bus station by hotel staff for transfer to your hotel. (Flight Kathmandu/Pokhara available at additional cost) Overnight at hotel ; (B,)

Day 04 In Pokhara

Your half-day sightseeing tour of Pokhara includes Pokhara bazaar, Phewa Tal Lake, David’s Fall, Gupteshwor Cave, Tibetan handicrafts & refugee center and Seti Gorge. One hour boating excursion on Phewa Tal Lake included. Overnight hotel ; (B)

Day 05 Pokhara – Chitwan (160km-6hrs Drive)

After breakfast (included), drive to Chitwan. Be greeted by staff at bus station and transferred to your hotel. Overnight hotel. Enjoy your included 3 course dinner. (B,L,D)

Day 06 Chitwan Jungle Safari

Enjoy a full day of jungle activities including elephant safari, nature walk, bird watching, jungle drive, Tharu stick cultural dancing, and more. If we are especially lucky, we will see the increasingly rare Bengal tiger. Sightings of the one-horned rhinoceros are also possible. Overnight hotel; (B,L,D)

Trip Information

Accommodation

Everest Base Camp Trek accommodation will be basic tea houses for boarding/lodging. This is the most popular style of trekking to Everest Base Camp and simply involves trekking from teahouse to teahouse. Teahouses are essentially small hotels find in local villages that offer both a place to sleep as well as home cooked meals. Rooms are typically shared with 2 trekkers in a room. Bathrooms are shared as well and in the Everest Base Camp Trek, the lodges usually have running cold water and western style toilets. Also the hot shower is also available but it need to pay extra cost for it.

In some of the villages on the trek, it may be possible to have a private room with an extra cost and we will do our best to arrange that. However, during busy times of the year there is a limited amount of lodging available in some of the villages so it's generally impossible to arrange private rooms.
